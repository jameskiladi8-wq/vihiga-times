Vihiga's 'Iron Lady' Stuns Region With Unforgettable Final Ride

28 Mar 2026 4 min read By Vihiga Times

VIHIGA, KENYA — Residents of Vihiga are still talking about a dramatic and emotional scene that unfolded on their streets this March—one that has quickly spread across Western Kenya and sparked widespread conversation online.

In a moment few had ever witnessed before, a young woman confidently rode a motorcycle through town while transporting a full-sized coffin carrying a body. Even more striking, a man sat firmly atop the casket as the motorbike weaved through the busy roads, creating a spectacle that left onlookers stunned.

Defying Norms in the Boda Boda Industry

The boda boda sector in Vihiga, like much of Kenya, has long been dominated by men. But this bold rider—now widely referred to as the region's "Iron Lady"—has challenged that status quo in the most unexpected way.

Witnesses described her as calm, skilled, and fully in control despite the unusual and demanding task.

"This is something we've never seen," said one resident. "She didn't just ride; she proved that women can handle even the toughest jobs out here."

Her actions have since become a symbol of resilience and changing gender roles, inspiring many across the region.

A Cultural Send-Off, Not a Stunt

While the scene may have appeared shocking to outsiders, within the boda boda community, it carries deep meaning.

The act of transporting a fallen rider on a motorcycle is often seen as a final tribute—a symbolic "last ride." The man seated on the coffin is typically a close friend or fellow rider, honoring the deceased by accompanying them on their final journey.

This tradition reflects the strong sense of unity within the boda boda fraternity, where members often go to great lengths to celebrate the life of one of their own.

Echoes From Kakamega

The incident has drawn comparisons to a similar viral moment in Kakamega in late 2025, where a woman gained national attention for transporting a coffin on a motorcycle. However, the added element of a man riding atop the casket in the Vihiga case has elevated the spectacle to a whole new level.

Mixed Reactions, Powerful Impact

As videos of the event continue to circulate, reactions have been mixed. Some have raised concerns about safety, while others have praised the courage and symbolism behind the act.

For many, however, the moment represents something bigger—a shift in culture, a bold statement on gender equality, and a testament to the evolving traditions within local communities.

In Vihiga, one thing is clear: this "Iron Lady" has not only made history but has also sparked a conversation that will be remembered for a long time.
