Vihiga Mourns Victor Gulavi, Killed in Burnt Forest Road Accident While on Duty

A Burnt Forest road collision involving two oil tankers killed Victor Gulavi, a guard from Zivi Village. His body has been moved to Vihiga County Funeral Home as family prepares burial.

13 July 2026 4 min read By Vihiga Times Reporter Updated 10:00 AM

Residents of Zivi Village in Kegoye Sub-location are mourning the tragic death of Victor Gulavi, the eldest son of George and Redempta Agalomba, who lost his life in a road accident at Burnt Forest on Saturday morning.

According to information from family members and residents, Victor was on duty alongside a colleague guarding a stranded oil tanker when another oil tanker approaching the scene reportedly lost control and rammed into them.

Victor sustained fatal injuries in the collision and died at the scene. His colleague also sustained injuries and was taken for medical treatment.

On Monday, Victor's body was transferred from Eldoret to Vihiga County Funeral Home, where it will be preserved as the family makes arrangements for his burial.

Community in Mourning

The news of Victor's death has cast a cloud of grief over Zivi Village and the wider Kegoye community. Family members, friends and neighbours gathered at the funeral home to receive his body, describing him as a hardworking, humble and dedicated young man whose life was cut short while carrying out his duties.

Many residents say his death is a painful loss not only to his family but also to the community that watched him grow into a responsible and respected member of society.

Police Investigation Underway

Police officers attended the scene after the crash and have launched investigations to establish the circumstances that led to the accident.

By the time of publication, authorities had not released an official report detailing the cause of the crash or provided further information regarding the condition of Victor's colleague.

Renewed Road Safety Concerns

The fatal accident has once again drawn attention to road safety along the Burnt Forest highway, a section that has witnessed numerous serious crashes involving heavy commercial vehicles over the years.

Road Safety Measures Demanded
  • Improved traffic control around broken‑down vehicles on highways.
  • Better warning signs and reflective barriers for stranded trucks.
  • Faster emergency response times to reduce secondary collisions.
  • Enhanced enforcement of speed limits and driver fatigue regulations.

Residents and motorists continue to call for stronger safety measures, including improved traffic control around broken‑down vehicles, better warning signs and faster emergency response to reduce the risk faced by roadside workers and other road users.

Burial Preparations Continue

Victor's family is now preparing for his final send‑off, with burial arrangements expected to be announced in the coming days.

As relatives, friends and neighbours continue to mourn, many have expressed their condolences to the Agalomba family and prayed for strength during this difficult period.

How You Can Help

  • Contribute to funeral expenses through community fundraising efforts.
  • Share road safety awareness to prevent similar tragedies.
