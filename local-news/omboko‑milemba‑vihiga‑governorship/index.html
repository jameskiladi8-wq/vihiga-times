Omboko Milemba Confirms Vihiga Governorship Race, Dismisses Withdrawal Claims

Emuhaya Member of Parliament Omboko Milemba has dismissed reports that he has withdrawn from the Vihiga governorship race, saying he remains fully in the contest and ready for United Democratic Alliance (UDA) party primaries ahead of the 2027 general election.

20 May 2026 5 min read By Vihiga Times Reporter Updated 2:30 PM

Emuhaya Member of Parliament Omboko Milemba has publicly rebuffed claims that he has stepped down from the race for Vihiga County governor, saying his plans for leadership at the county level remain unchanged.

The MP has dismissed reports that he has withdrawn from the Vihiga governorship race. Milemba insists he is fully in the contest and is preparing to face the United Democratic Alliance (UDA) party primaries. He has also urged supporters to rely on official channels instead of social media speculation.

Official Response

Speaking to local media and political observers, Milemba said the rumors about his withdrawal were misleading and did not reflect his position. He clarified that he remains committed to the UDA ticket and is ready to compete in the nomination process.

The MP has engaged in meetings and public forums across Vihiga, where he has reiterated his interest in a county leadership role. Political analysts note that his clarification comes as the 2027 general election draws closer and party structures gear up for internal nominations.

Why This Matters

Omboko Milemba's reaffirmation of his gubernatorial ambitions is significant for Vihiga County politics, especially within the ruling UDA party. As Emuhaya MP, he brings a strong parliamentary profile into the governorship race, which could influence how other aspirants position themselves.

The contest for the UDA ticket in Vihiga is expected to be competitive, with several names already being mentioned in local political circles. Milemba's declared focus on economic growth, education, infrastructure, and youth opportunities frames the debate around what kind of leadership Vihiga needs next.

Community Impact and Background

Vihiga County has drawn national attention in recent years for its economic profile and emerging political competition. Milemba has, in the past, publicly stated that he wants to be the "third governor of Vihiga come 2027," indicating a long-term plan for county leadership.

Political observers note that internal UDA dynamics, including the number of aspirants and party nominations, will shape the final line-up for the gubernatorial race. Local residents are watching how party leaders navigate these contests, as the outcome will affect resource allocation, projects, and service delivery in coming years.

Verifying Political Information

While this is a political story, the episode highlights the importance of going by verified information and not social media rumors. Residents are advised to rely on official statements from politicians, party structures, and county authorities when assessing candidacies and leadership changes.

Community leaders and opinion-shapers can help by correcting misinformation and encouraging calm, issue-based discussions around leadership.

What Happens Next

The stage is now set for deeper party campaigning and internal UDA processes that will determine who represents the party in the Vihiga governorship race. Party primaries and grassroots elections will play a key role in deciding which aspirant gets the UDA ticket.

Milemba has said he is preparing to present his vision for Vihiga when the time comes, signaling that policy messaging and public outreach will likely intensify in the coming months. As the 2027 general election cycle ramps up, attention will also shift to how other political parties position their candidates against the UDA nominee.

For now, Omboko Milemba maintains that he is "in the race to the end" and is ready for the political contest ahead, dismissing any suggestion that he has changed his position.
