KPLC Mbale Employee Dragged to Kisumu Court After Alleged KSh20K Bribe Deal in Vihiga

8 May 2026 4 min read By Vihiga Times Correspondent

Residents in Vihiga were left talking after a Kenya Power employee attached to the Mbale branch was taken to court over allegations of asking for a bribe from a local resident.

The incident, which began as an ordinary safety concern, quickly turned into a corruption investigation involving officers from the Ethics and Anti-Corruption Commission (EACC).

According to reports, the matter started after a rotten tree reportedly fell near electricity lines in the area, raising fears among residents over possible danger to nearby homes and members of the public. Concerned locals are said to have reported the issue to the relevant office, hoping quick action would be taken before the situation became worse.

However, residents claim that despite making reports, no immediate action was taken.

Fearing for their safety, the affected parties reportedly decided to seek help from a private contractor who later handled the work and restored the damaged section.

The Bribery Allegations

But the situation allegedly took another turn when the Kenya Power official later visited the scene and found that the repair work had already been completed.

It is alleged that the officer threatened to disconnect the electricity supply unless he was paid a large amount of money. Reports indicate that the initial demand was KSh50,000 before the amount was allegedly reduced to KSh20,000 after discussions.

Unknown to the suspect, the complainants had already contacted officers from the Ethics and Anti-Corruption Commission.

EACC Operation and Arrest

Investigators are said to have moved in quietly and monitored the situation before carrying out an operation that led to the arrest of the suspect on Thursday. Authorities claim the official was caught while allegedly receiving the money.

The suspect was later taken to Kisumu Central Police Station before being arraigned at the Kisumu Anti-Corruption Court on Friday.

During the court session, the accused denied the allegations linked to soliciting and receiving a bribe. The court later released him on a cash bail of KSh200,000 with a surety of a similar amount as investigations and court proceedings continue.

"Ordinary citizens should not be forced to pay extra money to receive basic services or protection."

Public Reaction

The case has since sparked reactions online, with many Kenyans calling for stronger action against corruption in public offices. Others also encouraged members of the public to remain careful whenever they are asked to make suspicious payments, especially in cash.

The incident now adds to the growing conversation around accountability and service delivery in public institutions, with many residents saying ordinary citizens should not be forced to pay extra money to receive basic services or protection.

Investigations into the matter are still ongoing.
