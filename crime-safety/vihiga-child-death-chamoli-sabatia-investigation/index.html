Vihiga Horror: Child's Death in Sabatia Takes Dark Turn After Postmortem Findings

27 Apr 2026 5 min read By Vihiga Times Correspondent

A cloud of sorrow still hangs over Chamoli village in Sabatia, Vihiga County, as new details continue to emerge in a case that has shaken the entire community.

What first appeared as a confusing and painful loss has now taken a darker and more disturbing turn.

According to a postmortem report, the two-year-and-four-month-old child whose death shocked residents weeks ago did not die naturally. The findings revealed that the child suffered a serious head injury caused by a blunt object, leading to death before the body was later buried.

The weight of this revelation has left many struggling to come to terms with what truly happened.

For days, the case has drawn attention not only because of the young age of the victim, but also because of the circumstances surrounding the incident. The child's father has been named as the main suspect in the case. However, he remains on the run, with security officers actively searching for him.

Authorities have confirmed that efforts to locate and arrest the suspect are ongoing. They are urging anyone with information that could help in the investigation to come forward.

Meanwhile, the child was laid to rest just days ago, in a burial filled with grief, silence, and unanswered questions.

A Family Shattered

For the family, the pain is difficult to describe. Losing a child is something no parent or relative is ever prepared for. But in this case, the pain is even heavier, shaped by confusion, shock, and the need for justice.

Within the Chamoli community, fear and disbelief have spread. Residents are struggling to understand how such an incident could happen so close to home. Many are now calling for justice, while others are urging stronger protection for children in vulnerable situations.

A Call for Child Protection

This tragic case has also raised wider concerns about child safety.

Children depend entirely on adults for care, protection, and love. When that safety is broken, the consequences are devastating—not just for one family, but for the entire community.

Leaders and residents are now emphasizing the importance of speaking up. If something feels wrong, it should be reported early. Silence, fear, or hesitation can sometimes allow dangerous situations to continue unnoticed.

"As the search for the suspect continues, one hope remains clear—that justice will be served, and that such a tragedy will never happen again."

For now, Chamoli mourns. A young life, full of innocence and potential, has been lost far too soon.

May the little one rest in peace.
