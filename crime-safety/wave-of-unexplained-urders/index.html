Silence and Shadows: Fear Grips Vihiga County as Murders and Attacks on Officers Raise Alarm

25 March 2026 6 min read By Vihiga Times Investigative Desk

A growing wave of brutal killings and bold attacks on law enforcement has cast a heavy shadow over Vihiga County, leaving residents anxious and authorities under pressure to restore order. From gruesome body discoveries in Mbale to the shocking disarming of a police officer, the region is facing one of its most unsettling security moments in recent history.

A Grisly Discovery Near the Heart of Town

The most disturbing case emerged in Mbale, just a short distance from the local police station. What began as complaints of a persistent foul smell quickly escalated into a horrifying revelation: two decomposing bodies hidden inside a septic tank behind a commercial building.

One of the victims was later identified as Humphrey Baranga, a casual laborer popularly known as "Rasta." Early investigations indicate the victims may have been killed elsewhere and dumped at the site to conceal evidence. The calculated nature of the act—and its proximity to a police station—has deeply unsettled residents and business owners alike.

Law Enforcement Targeted

In a separate and equally alarming incident, a police officer stationed at the Vihiga County Assembly was violently attacked while on duty. The officer was stabbed multiple times by unknown assailants who fled with his G3 rifle loaded with 20 rounds of ammunition.

The attack has sparked serious concerns over the safety of officers and the security of government installations. Despite ongoing investigations, the stolen weapon remains unaccounted for, raising fears it could be used in further criminal activity.

Tensions have also been rising in Tiriki East, where officers conducting operations against illicit brews have faced hostility from locals, including mob attacks. The incidents point to a troubling shift in public defiance toward law enforcement.

A Chilling Killing in Ebuyangu

In Emuhaya, residents of Ebuyangu are still reeling from the gruesome murder of a local milk vendor whose body was found decapitated, with the head missing.

The nature of the killing has fueled speculation about possible links to organized crime or long-standing disputes, though investigators have yet to confirm any motive. Fear has gripped the community, with many residents retreating indoors after dark.

Leaders Demand Answers

The rising insecurity has drawn the attention of Senator Godfrey Osotsi, who has called on the national government to act swiftly. Speaking in the Senate, he warned that the county risks becoming a haven for criminal networks if decisive action is not taken.

"We cannot allow Vihiga County to become a breeding ground for criminal elements. The government must deploy adequate resources to protect both our officers and civilians," Osotsi stated.

Security Response Underway

Authorities have begun rolling out a series of measures aimed at curbing the violence:

  • Property Inspections: Joint operations with county officials to inspect buildings, septic tanks, and abandoned structures suspected of being used to conceal crimes.
  • Targeted Operations: Intelligence-led crackdowns to recover the stolen firearm and dismantle criminal networks.
  • Officer Protection: Plans to strengthen support and backup for local administrators and officers operating in high-risk areas.

Community Urged to Stay Alert

As investigations continue, residents across Vihiga County are being urged to remain vigilant and report any suspicious activity. Authorities emphasize that public cooperation will be critical in restoring safety and preventing further incidents.

Unusual nighttime movements, strange odors near buildings, or individuals in possession of unexplained weapons should be reported immediately.

Emergency Contacts

  • DCI Hotline: 0800 722 203
  • Police Emergency Lines: 999 / 911 / 112
  • Nearest Police Station: Residents are encouraged to report concerns directly to their local station

As fear lingers, one message stands clear: restoring peace in Vihiga County will require both decisive security action and a vigilant, united community.
