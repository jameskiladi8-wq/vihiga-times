98-Year-Old Woman Dies in Suspected Candle Fire at Home

10 May 2026 3 min read By Vihiga Times Correspondent

A family in Kimaeti, Bungoma County, is mourning after a tragic house fire claimed the life of a 98-year-old woman late Friday night.

The elderly woman, identified as Damaris Makuku Okeno, died after flames broke out inside a three-bedroom permanent house in the Bishop Uwezo area of Kimaeti Sub-County. According to police reports, the fire destroyed household items in one of the rooms before spreading rapidly and filling the house with thick smoke.

Authorities confirmed that the home was not connected to electricity. Preliminary findings suggest that a candle may have been the source of the fire. Investigators believe the candle had been lit to provide light inside the room after it was taken there earlier in the evening.

Reports indicate that the deceased's daughter had visited her mother and brought food. During the visit, a candle was allegedly placed on top of a non-functional television set positioned on a wardrobe drawer. It is suspected that the candle may have fallen onto clothing stored inside the wardrobe, igniting the blaze.

Once the fire started, it spread quickly through the room. Neighbours said they noticed smoke and raised an alarm, prompting attempts to rescue the elderly woman. However, by the time help arrived, the situation had already worsened, and she could not be saved.

Police Response and Investigation

Police officers who responded to the scene confirmed that part of the house had been severely damaged. They documented and processed the area before transferring the body to Kocholia Sub-County Hospital mortuary, where it awaits a postmortem examination.

Residents described the incident as heartbreaking, especially because the victim was advanced in age and reportedly spending her final years at home with family. The tragedy has also renewed concerns about the use of open flames inside homes, particularly in houses without electricity.

"This is the kind of pain that lingers. A life that gave so much... gone at the very moment it gave even more."

Community Concerns

Authorities have said investigations are ongoing to establish the exact cause of the fire. While early indications point to a candle, officials emphasized that findings will be confirmed after further examination.

The incident serves as a painful reminder of how quickly domestic fires can turn deadly, especially during the night when people are asleep and visibility is low.

For the family, the loss has left a deep emotional gap, as relatives and neighbours gather to offer support during this difficult time.

Rest in peace, Damaris Makuku Okeno.
